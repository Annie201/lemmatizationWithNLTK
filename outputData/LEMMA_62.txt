Google launch the Google Nexus 7 If tablet had lids, Google would’ve just lifted the one on it new Android tablet, the Google Nexus 7.
The announcement confirms what we’d already spotted when leaked pic of the new tablet, built by Asus, surfaced earlier in the week.
The 340g, 7in slate’s got a quad-core Tegra 3 hitched up to 1GB RAM to drive the all-new Jelly Bean (Android 4.1) OS to it 1200x800 IPS Gorilla Glass screen.
The Google Nexus 7 will be available in mid-July, with pre-orders from the Google Play Store.
Prices start at £160 for the 8GB model.

iPhone Jeans – a crime against fashion?
If you re constantly having to stand up just to slip your smartphone out of your pocket then you ve got two option – stop cutting off your circulation with skin-tight jeans, or slip on this made-for-iPhone pair.
Alphyn s Delta415 Wearcom jean feature a transparent plastic pocket for your iPhone (or similarly-sized smartphone), allowing you to check your notification and message at a glance without having to slip it out of your pocket.
Available in size 28 to 38, the Delta415 s can be snapped up for now for £100 so go forth and prepare to look fabulous, you stylish gadgeteer you.
YouView internet TV launch next week?
YouView internet TV launch next week?
YouView, the Freeview box-styled internet TV player, ha been in the work since 2010 and may finally launch on Wednesday, July 4th.
Funded by the taxpayer and Sir Alan Sugar, YouView is a set top box that offer streaming internet TV to those without a connected console or smart TV.
It’ll mean your big screen can play BBC iPlayer, Lovefilm, ITV Player, 4oD, and more.
But will there be something extra?
We’re hoping for Skype chat with a built-in camera, social networking with live TV, and recording internet TV to name but a few.
iPhone 5 to get NFC for Passbook payment We ve heard the iPhone 5 NFC rumbling before but this time the evidence of Cupertino landing some sort of iWallet in our lap at the (probable) iPhone 5 launch in October look pretty convincing.
If Apple doe stick NFC in the iPhone 5, it could be the start of a mobile payment revolution.
Retailers have been slow to foot the bill of NFC equipment in shop but if million of iPhone owner start demanding the right to pay with a swipe of their smartphone, we are sure to see this technology in our high street soon.
Google’s Project Glass future spec Sergey Brin took to the Google I/O keynote stage and turned it into a temporary catwalk to model the company’s latest eyewear – Project Glass.
We got to see three style – blue, white and black – each housing a “powerful” CPU in one arm, plus a mic, speaker, camera and touchpad.
The spec should be available in 2013, however you can pre-order a pair now for the princely (though, all thing considered, not astronomical) price of US$1500.
